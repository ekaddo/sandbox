About Walter T. Johnson III

Walter T. Johnson III is a dedicated community leader with deep roots in Greensboro. A native of our city and proud graduate of both Dudley High School and North Carolina A&T State University, Walter has devoted his life to serving and strengthening our community.

Personal Background

  • Greensboro Native - Lifelong resident with deep community connections
  • Dudley High School Graduate - Educated in the GSO public school system
  • NCA&T Graduate - North Carolina A&T State University alumnus
  • Collegiate Baseball Player - Understanding the value of teamwork and dedication
  • Youth Coach - 16 years coaching youth basketball and soccer
  • High School Teacher - 20 years inspiring the next generation
  • Active Church Member - Providence Baptist Church
  • Entrepreneur - 30 years creating over $3M in economic development

Community Contributions

As an entrepreneur, Walter has promoted numerous events throughout Greensboro, including boxing events and high school football. He has served as a host for Friday night high school football, bringing the community together. His parents also served the Greensboro community, instilling in Walter the importance of public service and civic engagement.

Leadership Experience

  • 20 Years as High School Business Teacher
  • 3 Year Member - Guilford County Parks & Recreation Commission
  • 4 Years Experience in Affordable Housing
  • $3M+ in Economic Development as Entrepreneur

Campaign Platform

Walter is committed to creating positive change in District 7 and Guilford County:

  • Recruit Jobs with Livable Wages - Bring quality employment opportunities to our district that allow families to thrive
  • Increase Funding for Schools - Ensure our children have the resources and support they need to succeed
  • Invest in Youth Job Programs & Community Programs - Provide pathways for young people to develop skills and build their futures
  • Support Law Enforcement - Strengthen public safety and community security

Why Walter?

With 20 years of teaching experience, Walter understands the challenges our youth face. As an entrepreneur who created over $3 million in economic development, he knows how to create jobs. His work in affordable housing and on the Parks & Recreation Commission demonstrates his commitment to community improvement. Walter brings proven leadership, deep community roots, and a genuine passion for making Guilford County better.

How to Register to Vote

Online Registration

Register online at ncsbe.gov before the deadline. Must be completed by February 6, 2026 for the primary.

In-Person Registration

Visit your local Guilford County Board of Elections office or register through voter registration drives.

Same-Day Registration

Register and vote the same day during early voting (Feb 12-28) if you missed the registration deadline.

Voting Methods

Early Voting

February 12-28, 2026
Vote before Election Day at designated locations. Check guilfordcountync.gov for site hours.

Absentee Ballots

Request an absentee ballot to vote by mail. Ballots available starting January 12, 2026.

Election Day Voting

March 3, 2026 (Primary)
Polls open 6:30 AM - 7:30 PM. Bring valid ID to your assigned polling place.
